As a musician—I play the alto saxophone—I've always been fascinated by the intersection of art and technology. While my day job is deep in the world of cloud architecture and software security, my passion for music often inspires me to build projects that bridge these two worlds.

## The Challenge of Music Theory

Anyone who learns an instrument knows that music theory can be a significant hurdle. Reading notes in the treble clef is one thing, but becoming fluent in the bass clef is an extra challenge. It requires constant practice to quickly switch between them and read both accurately. To make this process more fun and effective, I decided to apply my skills as a developer.

## Introducing: The Fasleutel Music Note Quiz

I'm proud to introduce **[Fasleutel](https://gjoris.github.io/fasleutel/)**, an interactive web application I built to help musicians practice both the bass and treble clefs. It's more than a simple tool; it's a complete quiz experience.

### Rich Features

The application includes several modes to suit everyone's learning style:

*   **Practice Mode:** Learn at your own pace without any time pressure.
*   **Time Race:** Test your speed and accuracy by guessing as many notes as you can in 60 seconds.
*   **Sprint (10 Notes):** Try to answer 10 notes correctly as quickly as possible.

After each quiz in Time Race or Sprint mode, you get a **visual feedback report** showing your score and highlighting the notes you struggled with, so you know what to focus on.

### Technology

For the tech enthusiasts: the application is built with pure **Vanilla JavaScript** for the quiz logic, **HTML5** and **SVG** for dynamically drawing the notes, and styled with the **Bulma CSS framework** for a clean and responsive design.
